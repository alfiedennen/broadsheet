# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

The broadsheet project adopts the **Contributor Covenant**, version 2.1,
as its code of conduct. It sets a clear, widely-recognised baseline:
everyone interacting in the project's spaces — issues, pull requests,
discussions, and any other channels — is expected to be respectful,
constructive, and welcoming, regardless of background or experience.

The full text is the canonical source of truth:

→ **https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/**

By participating in this project you agree to uphold it.

## Scope

This applies within all project spaces, and also when an individual is
representing the project in public spaces.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behaviour that falls short of this
standard, please report it to the maintainer:

- Open a GitHub issue if the matter is not sensitive, **or**
- For anything sensitive, contact the maintainer privately —
  **[@alfiedennen](https://github.com/alfiedennen)** on GitHub.

Reports will be reviewed and handled as promptly and fairly as
possible, and the reporter's confidentiality will be respected.

## Enforcement

The maintainer is responsible for clarifying and enforcing this
standard, and may take any action they deem appropriate and fair in
response to behaviour they consider inappropriate, threatening, or
harmful — up to and including a temporary or permanent ban from
project spaces. The Contributor Covenant's enforcement guidelines (in
the linked text) inform how that judgement is exercised.
